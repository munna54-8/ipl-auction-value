IPL 2026: Are Players Worth What They're Paid?

Career IPL performance (runs + wickets, weighted) compared against real 2026 auction/retention prices for 19 players, to see who delivers the most impact per crore spent — and who doesn't.

Methodology & data notes "Impact score" = career IPL runs + (wickets × 20), a standard rough T20 batting/bowling equivalence. This favors bowlers and all-rounders somewhat, since a single wicket is weighted heavily — worth keeping in mind when comparing pure batters to bowlers. Auction prices are from public 2026 auction/retention reporting; players with conflicting figures across sources were excluded rather than guessed at, narrowing the dataset from 37 to 19 players with high-confidence pricing.

Key findings

  • Best value MS Dhoni (₹4Cr) delivers 1,360 value points per crore — nearly 3.3x the #2 player, Trent Boult (₹3.2Cr, 920/crore).
  • Worst value Nicholas Pooran (₹21Cr) ranks last at 109/crore, followed closely by Arshdeep Singh and Yashasvi Jaiswal — none are "bad" players, but their price doesn't match their output-per-rupee.
  • Marquee overpay Rishabh Pant, the most expensive player in IPL auction history (₹27Cr), ranks 16th of 19 on value — a real gap between market hype and statistical output.
  • Bowlers cluster high 4 of the top 5 value picks (Boult, Chahal, Jadeja, Narine) are bowlers or all-rounders — wicket-taking is comparatively cheap to acquire relative to marquee batting talent.
